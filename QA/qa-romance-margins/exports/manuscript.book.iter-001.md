# Margins of the Heart

an editor inherits part of a print studio and must collaborate with the designer who once abandoned the project and her trust

## Chapter 01 — First Fracture

Ana Vale reaches the winter book market, where craft, routine, and old tenderness keep rubbing against unspoken hurt. Routine hides emotion because work must keep moving. Nico Arden reads the room too carefully, while Rosa Bell keeps alive the version of events other people would rather believe.
In the winter book market, shared work becomes harder to treat as neutral routine. Ana Vale tries to test whether honesty can coexist with collaboration, but habit keeps translating vulnerability back into strategy. Intimacy and self-protection keep taking turns as the wiser choice. An annotated recipe book changes the emotional meaning of the collaboration.
"You keep correcting the margins as if the page offended you personally." Nico Arden says. "The page is easier to repair than the person who left the proof half-finished." Ana Vale says.
Later, in a riverside print studio, a practical exchange opens an emotional fault line. Ana Vale tries to protect the shared work without retreating from feeling, but habit keeps translating vulnerability back into strategy. Professional clarity cannot contain emotional consequence. An annotated recipe book changes the emotional meaning of the collaboration.
"I can share the studio with you, but not if we keep pretending the distance is professional." Ana Vale says. "Then let the work be difficult for the right reason, not because we are still hiding." Nico Arden says.
Later, in the rooftop herb garden, care and defensiveness start moving in the same gesture. Ana Vale tries to say enough truth to keep tenderness from collapsing back into routine, but habit keeps translating vulnerability back into strategy. Desire exposes what routine tried to keep manageable. An annotated recipe book exposes an earlier act of care that both characters misread.
"You keep correcting the margins as if the page offended you personally." Nico Arden says. "The page is easier to repair than the person who left the proof half-finished." Ana Vale says.
Nothing that has opened here will close easily. No one can yet tell whether honesty will deepen the bond or expose its limits.
