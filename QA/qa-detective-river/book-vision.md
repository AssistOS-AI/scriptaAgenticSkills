# Book vision

## Core idea

- Title: The River Knows First
- Book ID: qa-detective-river
- Baseline profile: detective-police
- Work form: novelette
- Brief: A homicide inspector suspects that a drowning ruled accidental hides a municipal murder and a police cover-up.

## Short story

Inspector Daria Muntean notices forensic details that make a river drowning impossible and begins following a trail of missing evidence bags, duplicated maintenance logs, and erased camera footage. With diver-reporter Teo Sava she traces the cover-up to a redevelopment deal protected by precinct chief Pavel Orban. The investigation ends when Daria projects the recovered footage inside her own briefing room, turning the police station into the final crime scene.

## Author-specified characters

- Daria Muntean - homicide inspector; wants to prove the victim was killed before he entered the river lock; needs to accept that procedure can become the crime's accomplice; fears that her own precinct archived the decisive evidence.
- Teo Sava - diver turned reporter; wants to recover the missing lock-camera drive; needs to stop mistaking cynicism for safety; fears being dragged back under police orders.
- Pavel Orban - precinct chief and pressure figure; wants the file closed before the riverfront contract is signed; fears a chain-of-custody break that points to his desk.

## Author-specified scenes

- Autopsy against the report - the body shows blunt-force trauma hidden beneath water damage.
- Camera gap at Lock Six - Daria and Teo follow a maintenance override that exists twice with different timestamps.
- Diversion beneath the spillway - the hidden drive case leads them to the abandoned control room.
- Briefing room breach - Daria exposes Pavel during an internal briefing and streams the evidence live.

## Author-specified locations

- The river evidence hangar beside Lock Six - diesel sheen, wet rope, fluorescent glare; facts survive here only if they can be tagged faster than they are reassigned.
- The abandoned sluice control room above the spillway - iron rust, mossed concrete, drowned electrical hum; the city's hidden mechanism for deciding what gets flushed away.

## Author constraints

- Any river death inside the redevelopment corridor is reviewed first by the precinct and only later by the civil prosecutor.
- Procedural errors let the redevelopment board void testimony and delay charges indefinitely.
- Maintenance overrides can be audited only if their physical hardware is recovered from the lock site.
- Stakes: the victim's true cause of death, the integrity of the precinct, and the city contract tied to the riverfront.
