# The River Knows First

A homicide inspector suspects that a drowning ruled accidental hides a municipal murder and a police cover-up.

## Chapter 01 — First Fracture

Niko Dane reaches the flooded riverside promenade, where the case still smells of river water, wet paper, and municipal panic. Authority is visible in every locked cabinet and withheld file. Soriel Maren reads the room too carefully, while Cassia Kepler keeps alive the version of events other people would rather believe.
In the flooded riverside promenade, a procedural detail refuses to stay procedural. Niko Dane tries to lock the evidence to a timeline that cannot be denied, but the official record still protects the version that serves power. Evidence and authority now point in opposite directions. A falsified autopsy report destabilizes the official timeline.
"If the ledger was complete, why is the river camera blind exactly when the body entered the water?" Niko Dane says. "Because someone upstream decided the gap was safer than the truth." Soriel Maren says.
Later, in the civic tribunal annex, fresh evidence makes the official timeline wobble. Niko Dane tries to pressure the witness chain into coherence, but the official record still protects the version that serves power. Procedure protects the wrong people more efficiently than it protects truth. A falsified autopsy report destabilizes the official timeline.
"Then stop calling this a case file and say who profits if it stays closed." Soriel Maren says. "If the transcript holds, the city itself becomes part of the murder." Niko Dane says.
Later, in the evidence archive, the safe reading of the case begins to collapse. Niko Dane tries to force the official story to absorb one unmanageable fact, but the official record still protects the version that serves power. The clue chain becomes dangerous precisely because it is coherent. A falsified autopsy report links the victim directly to the protected redevelopment file.
"If the ledger was complete, why is the river camera blind exactly when the body entered the water?" Niko Dane says. "Because someone upstream decided the gap was safer than the truth." Soriel Maren says.
Nothing that has opened here will close easily. No one can yet tell whether proof will survive the institution built to absorb it.
