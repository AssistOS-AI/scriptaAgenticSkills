# The River Knows First

an inspector reopens a drowning case after evidence suggests the victim died while uncovering municipal corruption

## Chapter 01 — First Fracture

Darius Vale reaches the flooded riverside promenade, where the case still smells of river water, wet paper, and municipal panic. Authority is visible in every locked cabinet and withheld file. Mira Rowan reads the room too carefully, while Matteo Voss keeps alive the version of events other people would rather believe.
In the flooded riverside promenade, a procedural detail refuses to stay procedural. Darius Vale tries to lock the evidence to a timeline that cannot be denied, but the official record still protects the version that serves power. Evidence and authority now point in opposite directions. A sealed witness transcript destabilizes the official timeline.
"If the ledger was complete, why is the river camera blind exactly when the body entered the water?" Darius Vale says. "Because someone upstream decided the gap was safer than the truth." Mira Rowan says.
Later, in the evidence archive, fresh evidence makes the official timeline wobble. Darius Vale tries to pressure the witness chain into coherence, but the official record still protects the version that serves power. Procedure protects the wrong people more efficiently than it protects truth. A sealed witness transcript destabilizes the official timeline.
"Then stop calling this a case file and say who profits if it stays closed." Mira Rowan says. "If the transcript holds, the city itself becomes part of the murder." Darius Vale says.
Later, in the civic tribunal annex, the safe reading of the case begins to collapse. Darius Vale tries to force the official story to absorb one unmanageable fact, but the official record still protects the version that serves power. The clue chain becomes dangerous precisely because it is coherent. A sealed witness transcript links the victim directly to the protected redevelopment file.
