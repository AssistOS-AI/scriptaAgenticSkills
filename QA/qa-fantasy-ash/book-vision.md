# The Ash Keeps Your Name

## Core idea
Inherited protection becomes monstrous once it depends on ritualized theft from the people it claims to save.

## Short story
Exiled heir Neris Vale returns to Drovna when the valley's oath-fires begin speaking the names of the dead aloud. Brother Caelan Vey guides her to the furnace catacombs, where the family vow is revealed as a system that burns witness memory into obedience. Regent Maeron Vale insists that breaking the rite will leave the valley open to winter spirits. Neris must decide whether a protection built on coerced forgetting deserves to survive.

## Story question
Can Neris free the valley from her family's vow without leaving it defenseless against the cost the vow once contained?

## Dilemma
Keep the inherited oath that protects the valley through coerced obedience, or break it and let both truth and danger return together.

## Main characters
- **Neris Vale** - oath-breaker heir; outward goal: learn the true wording of the family vow and sever it; hidden need: accept that returning home requires losing the shelter her lineage once guaranteed.
- **Brother Caelan Vey** - disgraced oath-keeper; outward goal: guide Neris to the furnace catacombs; hidden need: stop mistaking custodianship for innocence.
- **Regent Maeron Vale** - temporary ruler of the valley; outward goal: bind a successor before winter spirits test the wards; hidden need: believe domination still counts as protection.

## Main locations
- **The ash terrace above Drovna village** - cedar smoke, soot under snowlight, muffled bells; belonging here is inseparable from visible obedience.
- **The vow furnace catacombs** - ember breath, iron prayer chains, ash under candle wax; legitimacy is literally burned into permanence here.

## Key scenes
1. **Ash speaks the forbidden name** - the oath-fires whisper the name of Neris's supposedly dead sister.
2. **Chronicle beneath the chains** - furnace records reveal that the vow consumed memory as well as blood.
3. **Warding breach above the terrace** - the valley's protection fails just enough to prove the ritual cost was real.
4. **Breaking the furnace vow** - Neris names the dead publicly and lets the stored memories flood the terrace.

## Constraints
- Institution: **the House of Vale**
- Plot object: **the binding furnace seal**
- Motif: **ash carrying unspoken names**
- World rule: the valley wards hold only while an heir keeps the furnace vow alive.
- Cost: every renewal of the vow consumes memory, bloodline truth, and public freedom together.
- Hard pressure: Neris cannot expose the rite without risking both the valley's safety and her family's legitimacy at the same time.
